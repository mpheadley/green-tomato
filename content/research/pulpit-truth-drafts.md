## 1. The Shepherd App

**Phase:** 2 / 3 (sharper, institutional — spiritual abuse satire)
**Status:** Complete, ready to publish
**Format:** Article, ~350 words

**Headline:** Covenant Church Launches "Shepherd" App to Help Husbands Lead Their Wives Biblically

**Subhead:** *Jacksonville megachurch says new technology promotes spiritual accountability in marriages*

**Body:**

Covenant Community Church unveiled its latest digital initiative Tuesday: the Shepherd app, a mobile platform designed to help husbands "biblically lead" their wives through real-time location tracking, text message monitoring, and call history review.

"We realized modern marriages need modern solutions," said Pastor Marcus Webb, Covenant's senior pastor. "Shepherd gives husbands the tools to lead with confidence and clarity. It's about transparency — wives knowing their husbands are invested in their spiritual growth."

The app, available on iOS and Android, allows husbands to view their wives' location at all times, receive alerts when their wives contact specific people, and review message threads. A premium tier includes email monitoring. The Shepherd dashboard displays a wife's daily schedule, calendar entries, and shopping history.

"My wife used to make financial decisions without consulting me," said Chad Philips, a Covenant member using the app for three months. "Now she knows I'm aware of every transaction. It's brought us closer."

Church leadership emphasizes the biblical foundation. "Ephesians 5 is clear — husbands are the head of the household," Webb said. "The Shepherd app simply digitizes that responsibility."

The church plans to recommend the app during premarital counseling and has partnered with three other regional churches to expand its reach. A family plan allowing fathers to monitor adult daughters is in beta testing.

When asked whether wives have input on the monitoring, Webb paused. "That's actually a great question," he said. "The short answer is — they don't need to. Trust means not asking questions."

## 3. Chick-fil-A Baptism

**Phase:** 1 (silly / Babylon Bee lane — warmer)
**Status:** Complete, ready to publish
**Format:** Article, ~300 words

**Headline:** Chick-fil-A Adds Baptism to Menu: Full Immersion Available Between Combo Meals for $4.99

**Subhead:** *Oxford, Alabama location piloting new Spiritual Wellness Initiative ahead of planned nationwide rollout*

**Body:**

Chick-fil-A announced Tuesday the launch of its new Spiritual Wellness Initiative, beginning with a baptism service available at participating locations for $4.99, or free with the purchase of any deluxe combo meal.

The Oxford, Alabama location will serve as the pilot site for the program, which company representatives say fills a gap in the market.

"We've always known our customers experience something spiritual here," said regional manager Todd Brinkley. "We just finally decided to make it official."

The baptism service, available during regular business hours except Sundays, features full immersion in a custom-built tank installed adjacent to the beverage station. Customers may choose between traditional Baptist full immersion, Presbyterian sprinkling, or what Brinkley describes as the "Charismatic Experience," which includes laying on of hands and complimentary waffle fries.

A laminated salvation card is included with every baptism. Upgrades are available.

"For $7.99 you get the baptism, a combo meal, and a letter of membership to our partner church," Brinkley said. "We call it the Eternal Life Value Meal."

Local pastors have responded with mixed reviews. Reverend Dale Worthy of Cornerstone Fellowship called the initiative "spiritually innovative," while the Reverend Gary Simmons of First Baptist Oxford expressed concern that the drive-thru option "cheapens the sacrament."

Chick-fil-A corporate confirmed the drive-thru baptism lane is currently in beta testing.

"We want to meet customers where they are," said a company spokesperson. "If where they are is a drive-thru, we believe God can work with that."

The Oxford location reports seventeen baptisms in the first week, making it the highest-performing spiritual wellness location in the Southeast. Three customers requested refunds.
