## Strong Candidates (not yet drafted)

### Anniston Star
The natural institutional rival for The Green Tomato. Buford and the Star cover the same county. Trent Wickliffe (Star photographer) is already a named character — injured at the Raptor Run. The dynamic: Buford treats the Star as a peer institution with the full gravity of competing wire services.

**Specific angles:**
- **Buford quotes the Star as a primary source** — "The Anniston Star first reported..." He cites them with solemn attribution. The Green Tomato covers the Star covering things. The Star gets no credit for being amused.
- **Star publishes a correction; Buford publishes correction of the correction** — one paragraph, institutional solemnity. "The Green Tomato stands by its original characterization."
- **Star wins Alabama Press Association award** — Buford issues a congratulatory statement. Full ceremony language. The award was for a photo of a parking lot opening. Buford notes it was "well-deserved recognition of the photographic challenges inherent in ribbon-cutting coverage."
- **Buford pitches the Star a story** — they decline. He runs it himself. With dateline. As though it were wire service. The story is about a pothole.
- **Trent Wickliffe follow-up** — Raptor Run photographer, previously injured, returns to field. Buford notes his recovery with warmth. Trent is unavailable for comment but his byline has reappeared.

**No personal relationship required** — the Star is a public institution. No pitching needed, no heads-up. The only risk is if the Star finds it and responds. That would be excellent.

- **Lucinda Talley:** Madam, ran the second-floor operation from a chair at the top of the stairs for 20+ years. Shot by police ~1919 (missed suspect, hit Lucinda).
