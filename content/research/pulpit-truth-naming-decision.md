# The Pulpit Truth — Naming Decision Arc

*Captured 2026-05-01 from session conversation. Records how we landed on "The Pulpit Truth" so future sessions can understand the reasoning, not just the conclusion. The conclusions are in `THE_PULPIT_TRUTH.md` §The Name; this doc is the why.*

*Twelve rounds. The arc went: LNN → LBN → metaphor exploration → kingdom family → KNN → The Pulpit (Round 11) → The Pulpit Truth (Round 12, locked).*

---

## Starting position

Initial brainstorm proposed **Liberty News Network (LNN)** as the brand. Three-letter network shape echoing CNN, "Liberty" carrying the religious-liberty / Liberty University freight.

## The arc

### Round 1 — Does LNN signal Christianity clearly?

The challenge: "Liberty" reads patriotic before Christian to a general audience. To a first-time visitor, LNN scans as Newsmax/OAN — wrong satirical target (conservative politics generally, not Christian nationalism specifically). The "Liberty University" association only loads for readers already inside the tradition.

**Counter-consideration:** for Christian-nationalism satire specifically, the patriotic-Christian ambiguity is on-theme. The whole project being satirized is the fusion. A name that embodies the fusion is doing satirical work.

### Round 2 — LBN as the institutional-Christian fix

Considered **LBN (Liberty Broadcasting Network)** to fix the recognition problem. Pattern-matches CBN/TBN/INSP for the target audience (progressive Christians, ex-evangelicals, mainline pastors who grew up adjacent to Christian broadcasting). Keeps the "Liberty" cargo without forcing it to do all the work alone.

Initial recommendation: LBN. Reasoning: delayed-reveal works better when the audience knows what kind of outlet they're looking at, then gets surprised by the content. LNN puts that recognition lift on the writing alone.

### Round 3 — Do we need a metaphor?

Sharp clarifying question: are we naming with register (Liberty) or with metaphor (like The Onion / Green Tomato / Babylon Bee)?

Same trade-off Matt won once already with **Green Tomato over The Calhoun Gazette** — see `CONCEPT.md` §"Why Green Tomato wins." Metaphor names carry pre-installed humor, pre-installed thesis, pre-installed visual ownability, and lower brand-collision surface. Register names borrow vocabulary.

Considered metaphor candidates that *also* sound like plausible real Christian outlets:
- **The Pulpit** — unit of church furniture, place authority speaks from
- **The Steeple** — institutional perspective
- **Plumb Line** — biblical (Amos), evangelical insider-coded
- **The Watchman** — Ezekiel-coded, "warning the culture"

### Round 4 — Christian flag direction

Surfaced as the strongest single insight: the Christian flag is the literal visual embodiment of the patriotic-Christian fusion the satire targets. Flag-metaphor names name the subject matter.

- **The Standard** — flag standard + journalistic standard + moral standard
- **The Liberty Standard** — keeps Liberty equity, adds flag/standard metaphor weight
- **Standard-Bearer**
- **The Banner** — already a real Christian Reformed Church magazine (collision)
- **The Pledge** — direct reference to the Pledge of Allegiance to the Christian Flag
- **Allegiance / The Allegiance**

### Round 5 — Kingdom family

Considered **KNN (Kingdom News Network)**, **Jubilee News Network**, **Faithful Flag News**.

- **KNN** — strong. "Kingdom" is the actual watchword of the Christian-nationalist movement (kingdom mandate, Seven Mountains, "advancing the kingdom"). Names the satirical target on the masthead, the same comic move as Babylon Bee naming the empire. Three-letter `[X]NN` shape is video-native.
- **Jubilee News Network** — wrong-fit. "Jubilee" is already progressive-Christian-coded (Jubilee 2000, liberation theology). Collapses delayed-reveal to immediate-reveal.
- **Faithful Flag News** — weakest. Names the flag directly so the metaphor loses lift; awkward grammar; no clean wordmark.

### Round 6 — Christian nationalism as through-line

Sharper argument: Christian nationalism isn't a single beat alongside megachurch culture / prosperity gospel / institutional racism / CCM / missions — it's the *theology underwriting all of them*. Megachurches function as parallel civic institutions. Prosperity gospel maps God-blesses-this-place onto American exceptionalism. Bible Belt commerce is Christian nationalism in fast food. CCM festivals slide into MAGA rallies because they were already running on the same theology.

If that's true, naming Kingdom on the masthead names the unified field, not narrowing to a sub-beat. KNN moved back to lead.

### Round 7 — Pastoral standing as primary asset

Counter: Matt is drawn to **The Pulpit** because he was a pastor for 10 years (later corrected: 20 years, M.Div Northern Seminary, UMC). The Pulpit isn't sentimental — it's strategically right. It names the chair Matt actually sat in.

Other names point at the *theology being critiqued*. The Pulpit points at the *institution being critiqued from inside*. That's the rarer satirical posture and the project's primary asset (the concept doc already named pastoral background as "the credibility... cannot be faked").

### Round 8 — Kuyperian gloss as audience signal

Aside: the conversation surfaced "Kuyperian dominion thinking" as jargon Matt hadn't encountered. That's data — Kuyperian thinking lives in seminary bubbles (Westminster, RTS, Gospel Coalition orbit, Acton Institute). It's the academic-evangelical wing of dominion theology, cousin to Seven Mountains (charismatic) and Reconstructionism (Rushdoony, more extreme).

If Matt hadn't heard it after 10–20 years pastoring, his audience hasn't either. The satire should run on vocabulary the audience actually hears (kingdom, dominion, biblical worldview, cultural engagement) and named institutions/figures (Gospel Coalition, Piper, Wilson, Metaxas, Baucham), not academic frameworks.

That's mild evidence *for* The Pulpit (pew-and-pastor's-office vocabulary) over Kingdom-network names (which lean toward seminary set).

### Round 9 — Writing strength → The Pulpit

Matt: "I'm stronger right now in writing than video." That update tilted toward The Pulpit. KNN's main remaining advantage was the broadcasting-network frame mapping to video; if writing is the strength, network-named brand creates expectations of video-first content the project can't yet meet. The Pulpit is magazine-coded by default.

### Round 10 — Video pivot

Matt: "Culture is video. Don't want to get left behind like the Onion. I could produce both."

The Onion's failure to translate to social-native video is the cautionary tale. Video-native frame matters strategically; can't optimize for current-moment skill at the cost of long-term brand fit.

That update tilted back toward KNN. **Synthesis proposed:** KNN as the brand umbrella, "From The Pulpit" as a flagship column or video segment within it. That's how real broadcast networks work — branded segments under a network roof. Preserves both the network frame (video-native) and the pastoral credential (segment-level).

### Round 11 — Final pick: The Pulpit

Matt: "Document the Pulpit as the lead answer now."

Five-point case for The Pulpit:

1. Names Matt's standing, not the satirical target — irreplaceable credential
2. Does GT-style comic work on first contact
3. Broadest metaphor for all content pillars (Christian authority generally, not just dominion theology)
4. Cleanest wordmark
5. Magazine-coded register fits writing-first phase

KNN-as-umbrella synthesis kept on the bench in case the project pivots video-first later.

### Round 12 — The Truth upgrade (locked)

Matt's argument: "The more seriously it takes itself, the funnier it is. That's the whole joke. The Onion doesn't wink at you. It presents absurdity with complete institutional gravitas and that's what makes it land. A publication called 'The Pulpit Voice' sounds like it knows it's an opinion outlet. A publication called 'The Pulpit Truth' sounds like it genuinely believes it has cornered the market on divine revelation — which is exactly the posture of the evangelical culture you're satirizing. The name itself is already the joke."

He's right. "The Pulpit" alone is a metaphor name with implicit authority register; "The Pulpit Truth" makes the satirical commitment explicit. Real evangelical-coded outlets adopt the truth-claim register: World Magazine's tagline was "sound journalism, grounded in facts and biblical truth"; The Federalist, PragerU, Charisma News, Newsmax all position around truth-telling as a brand claim. The Pulpit Truth slots into that register and parodies it by existing.

The "Truth" addition strengthens every prior argument:

- **Names the satirical posture explicitly** (was implicit in "The Pulpit") — the pomposity of claiming Truth is itself the satirical thesis on the masthead
- **Stronger real-outlet plausibility** — "The Pulpit Truth" sounds more like a real conservative-Christian outlet than "The Pulpit" alone
- **Same first-contact comic work** as "The Pulpit," with an added pomposity beat
- **Wordmark trade-off** — loses single-word cleanliness, but gains stacked-masthead weight (Pulpit large + Truth as sub-line, the same shape Christian Post and World Magazine use)

Trade-offs accepted:
- Longer URL (thepulpittruth.com vs. thepulpit.com)
- Two-word wordmark instead of single-word
- Slightly longer to type/say

Both are negligible against the satirical commitment "Truth" carries.

**Locked: The Pulpit Truth.** Domain: `thepulpittruth.com` preferred, `thepulpittruth.news` fallback.

---

## What this arc reveals

- The right brand depends on which axis is load-bearing: register vs. metaphor, Onion-edge vs. institutional-Christian gravity, name-carries-thesis vs. chrome-carries-satire, audience-recognition vs. writing-carries-the-lift, and (Round 12) **how seriously the name itself takes the satirical posture**.
- For The Pulpit Truth specifically, two axes were load-bearing: **personal credential** (the Pulpit half names Matt's 20 years pastoring — the project's primary asset) and **satirical commitment** (the Truth half names the posture being critiqued).
- Future pivots that might re-open the question:
  - If video becomes primary and the writing-strength argument inverts → revisit KNN with "From The Pulpit Truth" as a segment within
  - If the satirical center of gravity tightens specifically to Christian nationalism (rather than evangelical authority broadly) → KNN out-targets The Pulpit Truth
  - If the brand needs to scale beyond a single voice → network-shaped names (KNN) hold multiple bylines better than a metaphor-name

Don't re-litigate without one of those triggers.
