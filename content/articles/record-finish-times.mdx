ANNISTON — The Anniston Runners Club has confirmed that participants in Saturday's Raptor Run 5K registered the fastest collective finish times ever recorded at a Calhoun County road race, with the top three finishers all clocking under 14 minutes.

The course record, previously held by a 2019 Talladega College competitor at 16:47, was broken by an estimated 312 runners on Saturday. The new record of 11:23 was set by an unidentified runner who declined to be photographed at the finish line and was last seen continuing past the timing mat at full speed in the direction of his vehicle.

"These are world-class times for a recreational 5K," said Anniston Runners Club president Greg Tilman in a statement Tuesday. "What we saw on Saturday speaks to the dedication and athleticism of our community. Several of our regulars exceeded their personal bests by margins we haven't seen since the introduction of carbon-plate racing shoes."

When asked whether the conditions of the race may have contributed to the unusual times, Tilman noted that the course was, in his words, "highly motivating."

The Anniston Museum of Natural History, which hosted the event, offered a related statement praising what it called "the resilience and tenacity of Saturday's participants." A spokesperson for the museum confirmed that no runner has yet returned to the race site to retrieve personal items left at the gear check.

Calhoun County Commissioner Dale Morrow said the times reflected the strength of local fitness culture.

"This is what we're capable of as a community," Morrow said. "When we set our minds to something, there's no telling how fast we can move."

Several runners contacted for this story declined to comment. One, reached at his home in Oxford, said only that he was "fine" and asked that this reporter not call back.

Race results have been preserved in the Anniston Runners Club archive. Officials confirmed that no times will be voided, regardless of the circumstances of any individual performance.

*The Calhoun Dispatch will continue to monitor this developing story. Anyone with information regarding gear left at the Raptor Run is asked to contact the Anniston Museum of Natural History during regular business hours.*
