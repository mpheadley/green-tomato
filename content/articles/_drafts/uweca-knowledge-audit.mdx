ANNISTON, Ala. — Calvin Odom, President and CEO of the United Way of East Central Alabama, announced Monday the completion of his annual internal knowledge audit, a documentation initiative he began in his third year with the organization and has maintained without interruption since. The audit now spans four three-ring binders, contains an estimated 15,000 handwritten notes organized across four color-coded index systems, and weighs eleven pounds when transported together.

Odom described the project as "just good practice."

"If something happened to me tomorrow, someone would need to understand what I know," he said. "That person should not have to figure it out from scratch. That is not fair to them and it is not fair to the organization."

The binders cover program operations, donor relationships, community partnerships, staff development notes, and what Odom described as "a section on things that are harder to explain but that someone will need to know." That section is tabbed separately. It is the thickest section.

United Way staff were informed of the audit's completion at the Monday morning all-hands meeting. Two staff members said they had not known the audit existed. Three said they had suspected it existed. One said she had seen the binders once, in the back of Odom's car, and had not asked.

Odom said the documentation would be reviewed and updated on a rolling basis and should not be read as a signal of any kind. He said he intended to continue in his current role "for the foreseeable future" and that the binders were "not a goodbye."

He noted, however, that he had a few more things to add.

"Probably another section," he said. "I've been thinking about transitions. Not mine specifically. Just transitions in general. It's good to be ready."

The binders are stored in his office. The combination to the filing cabinet where they are kept has been written down and placed in a sealed envelope. The envelope is also in his office.

*United Way of East Central Alabama serves 13 counties across East Central Alabama. It has 32 staff members and nine social workers. It did not have any social workers when Odom started.*
