ANNISTON, Ala. — Calvin Odom, who oversees 32 staff members, nine social workers, and an operating footprint spanning thirteen Alabama counties as President and CEO of the United Way of East Central Alabama, noted in a recent interview that his professional career began at a Movie Gallery.

He was a college student at the time. He worked there while completing a marketing degree at Jacksonville State University. He described the job as useful.

"The late fees taught me something," he said.

He did not elaborate on what they taught him.

Odom left Movie Gallery after graduating and spent several years operating a graphic design business he had taken over from his wife, who had started it before most print shops offered in-house design services. He worked from an early Apple computer with eight megabytes of memory. He described working from home as isolating.

He joined the United Way staff for two part-time fall fundraising campaigns. He has not left.

The Movie Gallery closed in 2010 as part of a national chain liquidation. Odom said he drove by the building once afterward. He did not say what he thought about when he did.

He currently manages four binders of institutional documentation and a sealed envelope. The Movie Gallery did not have either of those things.

*United Way of East Central Alabama accepts donations year-round.*
