OXFORD, Ala. — The United Way of East Central Alabama concluded its annual Live United luncheon Monday at the Oxford Civic Center, with attendance exceeding 200 and what executive director Calvin Odom described as "the kind of room that reminds you why this work matters."

Odom was moved to tears during his prepared remarks. This marks the seventh consecutive year in which that has occurred.

The tears came approximately twelve minutes into a fifteen-minute address, at the point in Odom's remarks where he described the impact of United Way's social work programming on a family he did not name. Several attendees wiped their eyes. The Alabama Power table gave a standing ovation.

Odom recovered, completed his remarks, and laughed about it during the post-event reception.

"I knew it was coming," he said. "I always know it's coming and I can never stop it. That is the honest truth."

He was asked whether he had considered adjusting the remarks to avoid the passage that triggers the response each year.

"I've thought about it," he said. "But the passage is accurate. And it should be said. If it makes me cry, that's what it does."

Attendees described the moment as expected. Brian Howell, who serves on the United Way board, said the tears had become "part of the program" and that he personally found them "appropriate." A first-time attendee seated at the Eastman Chemical table said she had not been warned and was glad she hadn't been.

Odom said next year's event would be held at the same venue, with the same format.

He was asked whether he expected to cry again.

"Almost certainly," he said.

*The 2027 Live United luncheon date has not been announced. Advance registration is recommended.*
