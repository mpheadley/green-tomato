JACKSONVILLE — The Knox Concert Series, a fixture of Jacksonville State University's performing arts calendar for over six decades, announced Tuesday a comprehensive rebranding intended to expand the series' appeal to a broader regional audience. The fall 2026 season will open in September with a four-piece bar band from Talladega.

The series, traditionally devoted to chamber music, classical recitals, and visiting orchestral performances, will retain its existing name and venue. Programming, however, will reflect what series director Dr. Caroline Whitlock described as "an evolving relationship between the academy and the community we serve."

"Knox Concert Series has always meant excellence," Whitlock said in a statement Tuesday. "What that excellence looks like — what it sounds like, who it speaks to — has to evolve. We are committed to inviting our community in, and that begins with meeting that community where it is."

Where the community is, according to Whitlock's planning committee, includes a Talladega-based cover band called Wet Concrete; an Elvis impersonator from Cullman who performs under the name "Just Elvis"; a four-week residency by a regional hip-hop dance ensemble; and what Whitlock described as "a thoughtfully curated heavy metal showcase featuring three Jacksonville-area bands and one from Atlanta."

The traditional season-opening Mahler symphony will be replaced.

JSU President Dr. Marvin Tharpe, attending Tuesday's press availability, called the new programming "an important step toward making the arts accessible to the people of Northeast Alabama."

"We have heard from members of our community for many years that the Knox series, while excellent, did not always reflect their interests," Tharpe said. "We are responding to that feedback. We believe the people of Calhoun County will find this season to be both engaging and approachable."

Wet Concrete will perform Friday and Saturday, September 18–19. Tickets are not yet available, but a JSU spokesperson confirmed pricing would be "comparable to previous seasons" — a range of $35 to $90 for premium seating. The bar band will perform two ninety-minute sets nightly, with a fifteen-minute intermission. The university confirmed alcohol service would not be expanded beyond the existing campus restrictions.

Members of the JSU music faculty contacted for comment declined to be quoted, with two requesting that their names not be included in any reporting on the announcement. A third faculty member, who asked to remain anonymous, said only, "I don't have anything I'd like to put my name to right now."

The Just Elvis performance is scheduled for the night of October 31. The performer's website indicates a setlist that includes "Burning Love," "Hound Dog," and "an extended in-character monologue regarding the state of modern country music."

When asked whether the season's traditional subscriber base — which a 2024 university report identified as 87% over the age of 60 — was expected to renew, Whitlock said the series anticipated "natural attrition" but expected it to be "more than offset" by new audience growth.

The Knox Concert Series was established in 1959 and is funded through a combination of university endowment, ticket sales, and a longstanding annual contribution from the family of the late William and Beatrice Knox, the series' original benefactors. The Knox family declined to comment for this story.

Wet Concrete's lead singer, reached at his home in Talladega, said he was "honored" by the booking and that the band was preparing what he described as "a slightly more polished setlist than what we usually do at Hooters."

*The Green Tomato will continue to monitor JSU's evolving programming. Subscriptions for the 2026–2027 Knox Concert Series are now available at the university box office.*
