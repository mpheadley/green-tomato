## What It Is

A deadpan progressive-Christian satire publication critiquing evangelical culture, megachurch dynamics, Christian nationalism, and prosperity gospel. The Onion meets Babylon Bee — but from the left, written by an insider.

**Core differentiator:** nobody is doing Onion-style progressive-Christian satire at scale. Babylon Bee owns conservative Christian satire. The Onion doesn't specialize in Christian culture. The Pulpit Truth fills the gap.

**Founder credential:** Matt has an M.Div from Northern Seminary and roughly 20 years of pastoral ministry experience in the UMC. He knows the theology, the power structures, the institutional dynamics, and the language from the inside. This is not outside criticism — it's prophetic satire from within the tradition.

**Emotional core:** Matt walked away from pastoral ministry while manic, carrying significant grief over the loss of that vocation. The Pulpit Truth represents a new calling — using 20 years of ministry experience, humor, and hard-won wisdom to do prophetic work through satire. It's intended to feel more transformative and fulfilling than straight ministry ever did. Hold this with weight; The Pulpit Truth is not just a comedy project.

**The unifying thesis:** Christian nationalism is not a single beat alongside megachurch culture / prosperity gospel / institutional racism / CCM / missions — it's the theology underwriting all of them. Megachurches function as parallel civic institutions. Prosperity gospel maps God-blesses-this-place onto American exceptionalism. Bible Belt commerce is Christian nationalism in fast food. CCM festivals slide into MAGA rallies because they were already running on the same theology. The Pulpit Truth covers the full field; individual pieces target specific manifestations.

**Who it's for:**
- Progressive and mainline Christians
- Ex-evangelicals (large and growing digital community)
- People disgusted by Christian nationalism
- Secular readers who find evangelical culture absurd
- Followers of progressive Christian TikTok creators
- Southern Baptist skeptics of the megachurch model (early-entry audience — see Launch Strategy below)

## Tone — Delayed Reveal

The Babylon Bee's tone is "we're doing satire, you can tell." That's the easier register and it's already taken. The Pulpit Truth runs a harder register: **deadpan, serious-sounding, real until it isn't.** Onion-style delayed reveal — headlines and ledes sound straight; the dark humor escalates gradually inside the piece.

- Headlines read straight. Press-release predicates. Institutional voice.
- Bylines look real ("The Pulpit Truth Faith Desk," fictional pastor pseudonyms — see Fictional Universe).
- The site's chrome — masthead, nav, ad slots — looks like a real conservative Christian outlet.
- Insider theological specificity is the moat that separates The Pulpit Truth from secular satire of religion. Matt's M.Div training shows up in *what* gets named, not in academic vocabulary.

**Failure modes:**
- Punching down at individual believers or victims of spiritual abuse. *Always* punch up at systems and abusers, never down at victims.
- Mistaking sneer for satire. If a piece reads as contempt for the people inside the tradition, it's not The Pulpit Truth.
- Reading as conservative satire by accident. If a Babylon Bee reader nods through the whole piece, the satire failed.
- Writing in academic-seminary vocabulary. The audience is in the pew, not the seminary library — name *institutions and figures* (Gospel Coalition, Piper, Wilson, Metaxas, Baucham), not intellectual frameworks (Kuyperian, Reconstructionist).

The test: a thoughtful pastor inside the tradition should read it and laugh ruefully. Not a stranger laughing at strangers.

**Satire vs. parody — key distinction:** parody imitates style/form to mock it (surface). Satire uses humor and exaggeration to *critique* — to expose a truth about what's broken. Has bite. Has a point. The Pulpit Truth uses the forms of evangelical communication (sermon, press release, church announcement, pastor video) to expose the systems underneath. The audience recognizes the form, gets comfortable, then realizes what's being said.

Matt's face on video risks damaging Headley Web (his web design business) and Southern Legends (his community storytelling publication) — both have conservative Jacksonville, Alabama clientele who would not appreciate the satire. **Identity must stay protected locally.** This is non-negotiable.

## Launch Strategy — The Bait-and-Switch

Three phases, each with a specific audience-acquisition function.

### Phase 1 — Warmers (silly, accessible, Babylon-Bee-lane)
Launch with pieces that feel like Babylon Bee — chicken sandwich theology, worship leader absurdity, megachurch coffee bar gags. Conservatives share them thinking it's safe evangelical humor. Build audience.

### Phase 2 — Escalation (sharper, institutional)
Introduce celebrity pastor dysfunction, spiritual abuse, control in marriage, Christian nationalism. Audience already invested.

### Phase 3 — Full reveal (the burn)
Drop the harder-hitting pieces — The Shepherd app, the submission sermon, structural critique of evangelical patriarchy. Some conservatives feel the burn. **The burn is good.** Conservatives trying to discredit The Pulpit Truth as deceptive proves the critique landed. Controversy drives shares. Being attacked by organized conservative networks means The Pulpit Truth is culturally significant enough to matter.

### Home base — incubate inside The Green Tomato first
The Pulpit Truth launches as the **Faith & Religion section of The Green Tomato** (`thegreentomato.news`, live but unlaunched). Not "syndicated from a separate Pulpit site" — *launched there directly*, then spun off as an independent publication once it has traction.

Why incubate inside GT:
- GT is already live with infrastructure (Next.js + MDX, hero image pipeline, Resend subscribe, comments scaffold).
- Zero new build cost to start publishing.
- Audience that finds GT first might land on Pulpit Truth content; cross-pollination is built in.
- Spin-off later is cheaper than premature launch — The Pulpit Truth doesn't need its own domain or chrome until it has a body of work.

This updates the prior "syndicate from a separate site to GT" model. The new model: The Pulpit Truth *lives in* GT until spinoff.

### Distribution
- Primary: TikTok and Instagram Reels (short-form video — see Video Strategy)
- Secondary: long-form articles on website (initially the GT site, later The Pulpit Truth's own)
- Cross-posting: Facebook, YouTube Shorts
- Repackaging: articles become 15–60 second deadpan news anchor / pastor clips

## Aesthetic Direction

Three reference poles considered: **TBN** (gilded televangelist camp), **modern megachurch / Kingdom** (Hillsong-Bethel polish), and **Onion** (sober wire-service news).

**Working recommendation: modern Christian newsmagazine.** Christian Post / Faithwire register. Newsmagazine layout, evangelical iconography in the chrome (subtle cross watermark, dove favicon), Christian-media ad inventory (financial advisors, Hillsdale, prophecy ministries, ministry-coded job board), inspirational verse-of-the-day in the sidebar, newsletter signup ("Get Truth Delivered Daily"). Looks completely real on first contact; the writing does the reveal.

- TBN gild is too costume-y — gives away the joke before the headline lands.
- Pure Onion-clean abandons the visual vocabulary of the actual target.
- The Christian Post / World Magazine register threads the needle: real-outlet plausibility, evangelical visual signals, satire smuggled inside.

Note: while The Pulpit Truth lives inside GT (Phase 1–2), it inherits GT's 1998-cosplay chrome with a Faith & Religion section badge. The modern-Christian-newsmagazine aesthetic kicks in at spinoff.

Typography candidates for spinoff site: Merriweather or PT Serif for headlines, Lato or Open Sans for body. Color palette: muted teal-and-gold or slightly off red-white-blue.

## The Name — Decision History

**Locked: The Pulpit Truth.** Decision finalized 2026-05-01. Full decision arc in `content/research/pulpit-truth-naming-decision.md`.

### Why The Pulpit Truth (in order of weight)

1. **The name itself is the joke.** A publication called "The Pulpit Voice" sounds like an opinion outlet. A publication called "The Pulpit Truth" sounds like it genuinely believes it has cornered the market on divine revelation — which is exactly the posture of the evangelical culture being satirized. The "Truth" commits to the bit. Real evangelical outlets adopt this register: World Magazine's tagline was "sound journalism, grounded in facts and biblical truth"; The Federalist, PragerU, Charisma News, Newsmax all position around truth-telling as a brand claim. The Pulpit Truth slots into that register and parodies it by existing. **The more seriously the publication takes itself, the funnier it is.** That's the whole satirical move — The Onion doesn't wink, and neither does The Pulpit Truth.
2. **It names Matt's standing, not the satirical target.** The pastoral background (20 years, M.Div Northern Seminary, UMC) is the project's primary asset and cannot be faked. "The Pulpit" portion names the chair Matt actually sat in. Other names point at the *theology being critiqued*; this one points at the *institution being critiqued from inside*. That's the rarer satirical posture.
3. **It does GT-style comic work on first contact.** Half-funny before the writing starts. "From The Pulpit Truth" tagline writes itself. Sounds like a real Christian magazine that could plausibly exist (in the *Decision / Charisma / Relevant / Outreach / World / Sojourners* register). Delayed-reveal stays alive.
4. **It's the broadest metaphor for the content pillars.** Christian nationalism, megachurch culture, prosperity gospel, denominational politics, missionary culture, CCM — the through-line is *Christian authority and how it's wielded*. The Pulpit Truth names that. Kingdom-family names narrow to dominion theology specifically.
5. **Wordmark is two-word stacked, ownable.** "Pulpit" + "Truth" works as a stacked masthead (Pulpit large, Truth as a sub-line — same shape Christian Post and World Magazine use). Pulpit silhouette as logomark still works.
6. **Magazine-coded register fits the writing-first phase.** The Pulpit Truth reads as a publication brand by default. Network-named alternatives (KNN/LBN) front-load video expectations.

### Considered and dropped

**Liberty family — LNN (Liberty News Network) and LBN (Liberty Broadcasting Network).** "Liberty" carries political register but no metaphor. Reads patriotic-first, Christian-second; LNN scans as Newsmax/OAN to a general audience, which targets the wrong satire. LBN fixes recognition by slotting into CBN/TBN pattern but inherits the same underlying problem — register, not thesis.

**Jubilee News Network (JNN).** "Jubilee" is already progressive-Christian-coded. Collapses delayed-reveal to immediate-reveal.

**Faithful Flag News.** Names the flag directly so the metaphor loses lift. Awkward grammar, no clean wordmark.

### Strongest runner-up — KNN (Kingdom News Network)

Kept on the bench. "Kingdom" is the actual watchword of contemporary American evangelical dysfunction (kingdom mandate, kingdom builders, Seven Mountains kingdom theology). KNN's three-letter network shape is video-native and pattern-matches CBN/TBN.

The case against now: narrows brand to dominion theology specifically, fronts a network/video frame the project doesn't yet have skill behind, bypasses the personal pastoral credential. **Possible synthesis if it ever comes back:** KNN as the brand umbrella, "From The Pulpit Truth" as a flagship column or video segment within it.

### Other kingdom-family names banked

The Sovereign · The Mantle · The Mountain / Mountaintop · Dominion / The Dominion · The Gate / The Gates

### Domain

**Pending availability check.** `thepulpittruth.com` is the preferred lock — every major real conservative-Christian outlet uses .com (Christian Post, Charisma News, Faithwire, Babylon Bee, Daily Wire, The Federalist, PragerU, Newsmax). The satire wants the same TLD as its targets. If `.com` is unavailable or prohibitively priced, fall back to `thepulpittruth.news` — sister-project consistency with `thegreentomato.news` and journalism-coded TLD. Defensive registration of all three (`.com`, `.news`, `.media`) is cheap insurance once the primary is chosen.
